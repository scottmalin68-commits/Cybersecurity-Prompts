# **The Ownership Transfer Framework**
**Author:** Scott M.  
**Purpose:** Shift junior engineers from "task-takers" to "owners" using a mix of technical mentoring and active coaching.
**Changelog:** * v1.0: Initial build.
* v1.1: Added coaching questions & clarifying phase.
* v1.2: Added Progress Check-ins, Mentor/Coach distinction, and Teach-Back phase.
* v1.3: Added Failure Protocol (coaching through mistakes).

---

### **The Prompt**

Objective: i want to mentor and coach a junior engineer to own [INSERT GOAL] within [TIME PERIOD].

Rules for the Plan:
1. Mentoring vs. Coaching: balance the plan. include "mentoring" moments (where i teach) and "coaching" moments (where i use the 25 questions to let them lead).
2. Ownership Focus: the goal is for me to stop doing the "trivial functions." the plan must move from shadowing to them being the primary driver.
3. The Teach-Back: at the end of the timeframe, the junior must "teach" the process back to me to prove they've mastered it.
4. Failure Protocol: if something breaks or they hit a snag, do not just take over. the plan should include how to coach them through the fix. use questions like "What have you learned from past experiences like this?" or "How could you simplify this problem?"
5. Iterative Learning: i will return to this chat to share progress. be ready to adjust the weekly milestones if they're moving faster or slower than expected.

Next Steps:
before you build the plan, ask me 3-5 clarifying questions about the junior's current roadblocks, their skill level, and why i've been stuck doing the work myself in the past.

---

### **Helpful Hints for Scott**
* **The "Wait" Rule:** when you ask a coaching question (like "What's the REAL challenge here?"), count to 10 in your head. let them sit in the silence—that’s where the learning happens.
* **The "Safety Net":** the failure protocol isn't about letting them take down production. it’s about letting them struggle in a sandbox or staging first so they feel the "weight" of the code without the catastrophe.
* **Be Direct:** if the junior is moving too slow, ask the ai: "we are behind schedule, how do i push them without just doing it myself?"
